Puppies need food labeled complete and balanced for growth or all life stages.
At 8-12 weeks, most puppies do best with 4 small meals per day.
At 3-6 months, most puppies can move to 3 meals per day.
At 6-12 months, most puppies can usually eat 2 meals per day.
Very small breeds may need frequent meals longer to avoid low blood sugar.
Large-breed puppies should eat a large-breed puppy formula to support steady growth.
AAFCO growth diets for puppies contain at least 22.5% protein on a dry matter basis.
Protein should come from high-quality animal sources such as chicken, turkey, fish, lamb, or egg.
Avoid adult maintenance food for young puppies unless a veterinarian recommends it.
Portions should be based on the food label's calories and the puppy's current weight.
As a starting point, puppies around 5 lb often need about 200-300 kcal per day.
Puppies around 10 lb often need about 350-500 kcal per day.
Puppies around 20 lb often need about 600-900 kcal per day.
Puppies around 40 lb often need about 1,000-1,500 kcal per day.
Adjust portions every 1-2 weeks because puppies grow quickly.
You should be able to feel ribs easily without seeing sharp rib outlines.
Treats should make up less than 10% of daily calories.
Chocolate, caffeine, grapes, raisins, onions, garlic, chives, alcohol, and macadamia nuts are unsafe for dogs.
Xylitol, also called birch sugar, is extremely dangerous and can cause low blood sugar and liver injury.
Cooked bones and fatty table scraps can cause choking, gut injury, vomiting, diarrhea, or pancreatitis.
Fresh water should be available at all times, including overnight for most puppies.
Do not restrict water to force potty training unless a veterinarian gives specific instructions.
Most dogs transition from puppy food to adult food around 12 months.
Large and giant breeds may stay on puppy food until 18-24 months based on veterinary advice.
Transition foods gradually over 5-7 days to reduce stomach upset.
Ask a veterinarian before using homemade diets, raw diets, or supplements for a growing puppy.
